How to Forgive When It Hurts: Biblical Steps to Freedom

📅 January 2025 | ✝️ 10 min read

Forgiveness is one of the hardest commands Jesus gave us. When someone betrays you, abandons you, or abuses you, forgiveness feels impossible. But unforgiveness hurts YOU more than the person who wronged you.

Why Forgiveness is Non-Negotiable

📖 Matthew 6:14-15
"For if you forgive other people when they sin against you, your heavenly Father will also forgive you. But if you do not forgive others their sins, your Father will not forgive your sins."
Jesus connects our forgiveness of others directly to God's forgiveness of us. It's not optional – it's a command with serious consequences.

What Forgiveness IS and IS NOT

  • Forgiveness is NOT pretending it didn't hurt.
  • Forgiveness is NOT reconciling automatically (trust must be rebuilt).
  • Forgiveness is NOT forgetting (you'll likely remember).
  • Forgiveness IS releasing the debt – choosing not to punish.
  • Forgiveness IS a decision, not a feeling.
  • Forgiveness IS freedom for YOU.

Bible Verses to Help You Forgive

📖 Colossians 3:13
"Bear with each other and forgive one another if any of you has a grievance against someone. Forgive as the Lord forgave you."
The standard: forgive the way God forgave you. Think about how much God has forgiven you – and extend that same grace.
📖 Ephesians 4:31-32
"Get rid of all bitterness, rage and anger, brawling and slander, along with every form of malice. Be kind and compassionate to one another, forgiving each other, just as in Christ God forgave you."
Bitterness is poison. Paul says GET RID OF IT. Replace it with kindness and compassion.
📖 Matthew 18:21-22
"Then Peter came to Jesus and asked, 'Lord, how many times shall I forgive my brother or sister who sins against me? Up to seven times?' Jesus answered, 'I tell you, not seven times, but seventy-seven times.'"
Unlimited forgiveness. Not because the person deserves it, but because you need to let go.

5 Biblical Steps to Forgive

  1. Acknowledge the hurt – Don't minimize it. Tell God exactly how you feel. The Psalms are full of honest lament.
  2. Remember how much God forgave you – Your debt to God is infinitely larger than anyone's debt to you.
  3. Make a decision to release them – Say it out loud: "I choose to forgive [name] for [specific hurt]."
  4. Pray for them – Jesus said pray for your enemies (Matthew 5:44). This changes your heart.
  5. Repeat as needed – Forgiveness is often a process. You may need to choose it daily.
🙏 A Prayer for Forgiveness

"Father, I'm struggling to forgive. The pain is real. But I know that unforgiveness is hurting me more than them. Today, I choose to forgive [name]. I release them from the debt they owe me. I give my anger and bitterness to You. Help me to truly mean this – and to keep choosing forgiveness when the feelings return. Thank You for forgiving me when I didn't deserve it. In Jesus' name, Amen."
