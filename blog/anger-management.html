Biblical Anger Management

7 verses to calm your heart

Anger is one of the most destructive emotions – but it's also natural. The Bible doesn't say anger is a sin. It says "In your anger do not sin" (Ephesians 4:26).

📖 Ephesians 4:26-27
"In your anger do not sin. Do not let the sun go down while you are still angry."
Two rules: Don't sin when angry. Resolve anger quickly – don't let it fester.
📖 Proverbs 15:1
"A gentle answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger."
Your response determines the outcome. A gentle answer defuses tension.
📖 James 1:19-20
"Everyone should be quick to listen, slow to speak and slow to become angry."
Three speeds: Quick to listen. Slow to speak. Slow to anger.
📖 Proverbs 29:11
"Fools give full vent to their rage, but the wise bring calm in the end."
Anyone can explode. Wisdom is controlling it.
📖 Colossians 3:8
"Rid yourselves of all such things as these: anger, rage, malice, slander, and filthy language."
Anger is something to "rid yourself of" – like taking off dirty clothes.
📖 Ecclesiastes 7:9
"Do not be quickly provoked in your spirit, for anger resides in the lap of fools."
Quick anger is foolish. Take a breath. Count to ten.
📖 Matthew 5:22
"Anyone who is angry with a brother or sister will be subject to judgment."
Unforgiveness damages your own soul.
🙏 A Prayer for Anger

"Father, forgive me for letting anger control me. Give me self-control. Help me to be quick to listen and slow to speak. Heal the hurts underneath my anger. Amen."
